## Supervised Learning

- **Linear Regression Assumptions**
  - **Linearity**: The relationship between independent and dependent variables is linear.
  - **Independence**: Observations are independent of each other.
  - **Homoscedasticity**: Constant variance of errors.
  - **Normality**: Errors are normally distributed.
  - **Multicollinearity**: Independent variables are not highly correlated.
  - **Why it matters**: Violations can lead to biased or inefficient estimates.
  - **Common confusion**: Many candidates confuse multicollinearity with correlation among dependent and independent variables. The key difference is multicollinearity refers to correlation among independent variables.

- **Logistic Regression**
  - **Purpose**: Used for binary classification problems.
  - **Mechanism**: Uses the logistic function to model the probability of a binary outcome.
  - **Splitting Criteria**: Maximizes the likelihood function.
  - **Why it matters**: Provides probabilities and classifies observations.
  - **Red flag**: Confusing logistic regression with linear regression due to the use of a linear model in logistic regression.

- **Decision Tree Splitting Criteria**
  - **Gini Impurity**: Measures the impurity of a node.
  - **Entropy**: Measures the disorder or impurity.
  - **Information Gain**: Reduction in entropy after a split.
  - **Why it matters**: Determines the quality of the split and impacts the tree's performance.
  - **Red flag**: Misunderstanding that Gini and Entropy are interchangeable; they have different mathematical formulations.

- **Random Forest Bagging + Feature Subsampling**
  - **Bagging**: Bootstrap aggregating to reduce variance.
  - **Feature Subsampling**: Randomly selecting a subset of features for each split.
  - **Why it matters**: Increases model robustness and reduces overfitting.
  - **Complexity**: O(m log n) for training, where m is the number of trees and n is the number of samples.

- **Gradient Boosting Weak Learners**
  - **Weak Learner**: Typically a shallow decision tree.
  - **Mechanism**: Sequentially adds models to correct errors of prior models.
  - **Why it matters**: Improves model accuracy by focusing on difficult-to-predict samples.
  - **Red flag**: Confusing boosting with bagging; boosting is sequential, bagging is parallel.

- **XGBoost**
  - **Mechanism**: An optimized gradient boosting framework.
  - **Features**: Regularization, parallel processing, and tree pruning.
  - **Why it matters**: Offers high performance and efficiency.
  - **Complexity**: O(K * log n) for training, where K is the number of trees.

- **SVM Kernels**
  - **Linear Kernel**: Best for linearly separable data.
  - **Polynomial Kernel**: Captures polynomial relationships.
  - **RBF Kernel**: Captures non-linear relationships.
  - **Why it matters**: Choice of kernel affects the decision boundary and model performance.
  - **Red flag**: Misunderstanding that SVMs always require non-linear kernels.

- **KNN Distance Metrics**
  - **Euclidean Distance**: Straight-line distance.
  - **Manhattan Distance**: Sum of absolute differences.
  - **Minkowski Distance**: Generalization of Euclidean and Manhattan.
  - **Why it matters**: Distance metric affects neighbor selection and classification accuracy.
  - **Complexity**: O(n) for prediction, where n is the number of samples.

## Unsupervised Learning

- **K-means Elbow Method**
  - **Purpose**: Determine the optimal number of clusters.
  - **Mechanism**: Plot the explained variance as a function of the number of clusters and look for an "elbow."
  - **Why it matters**: Helps avoid overfitting or underfitting.
  - **Red flag**: Misinterpreting the elbow point; it is not always clear-cut.

- **DBSCAN eps/min_samples**
  - **eps**: Maximum distance between two samples for them to be considered as in the same neighborhood.
  - **min_samples**: Minimum number of samples in a neighborhood for a point to be considered a core point.
  - **Why it matters**: Determines the density threshold for forming clusters.
  - **Complexity**: O(n log n) for spatial indexing, where n is the number of samples.

- **PCA Variance Explained**
  - **Purpose**: Reduce dimensionality while retaining variance.
  - **Mechanism**: Projects data onto principal components.
  - **Variance Explained**: Proportion of total variance captured by each component.
  - **Why it matters**: Helps in selecting the number of components to retain.
  - **Red flag**: Confusing PCA with feature selection; PCA is a feature transformation technique.

## Regularization

- **L1 Sparsity**
  - **Mechanism**: Adds absolute value of coefficients as penalty.
  - **Why it matters**: Produces sparse models with feature selection.
  - **Red flag**: Misunderstanding that L1 regularization always improves model performance; it depends on the context.

- **L2 Weight Decay**
  - **Mechanism**: Adds squared value of coefficients as penalty.
  - **Why it matters**: Reduces model complexity and prevents overfitting.
  - **Red flag**: Confusing L2 regularization with L1; L2 does not produce sparse models.

- **ElasticNet**
  - **Mechanism**: Combines L1 and L2 penalties.
  - **Why it matters**: Balances between sparsity and regularization.
  - **Red flag**: Misunderstanding the balance parameter; it controls the trade-off between L1 and L2.

## Bias-Variance Tradeoff

- **Bias**: Error due to overly simplistic model assumptions.
- **Variance**: Error due to model sensitivity to small fluctuations in the training set.
- **Tradeoff**: Balancing bias and variance to minimize total error.
- **Why it matters**: Determines the model's ability to generalize.
- **Red flag**: Misunderstanding that reducing bias always improves model performance; it can increase variance.

## Ensemble Methods

- **Bagging**
  - **Mechanism**: Trains multiple models in parallel on bootstrapped datasets.
  - **Why it matters**: Reduces variance and improves stability.
  - **Red flag**: Confusing bagging with boosting; bagging is parallel, boosting is sequential.

- **Boosting**
  - **Mechanism**: Trains models sequentially, each correcting errors of the previous.
  - **Why it matters**: Reduces bias and improves accuracy.
  - **Red flag**: Misunderstanding that boosting always leads to overfitting; it depends on the model complexity.

- **Stacking**
  - **Mechanism**: Combines predictions from multiple models using a meta-learner.
  - **Why it matters**: Leverages strengths of different models.
  - **Red flag**: Confusing stacking with bagging or boosting; stacking involves a meta-learner.
