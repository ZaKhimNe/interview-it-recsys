## Text Preprocessing

- **Tokenization**
  - **Definition**: The process of converting a text into individual units called tokens, which can be words, phrases, or symbols.
  - **Mechanism**: Involves splitting text based on spaces, punctuation, or custom rules. Tokenizers can be rule-based or use machine learning models.
  - **Importance**: Essential for converting raw text into a format suitable for analysis. Incorrect tokenization can lead to loss of meaning or context.
  - **Common Confusion**: Tokenization vs. segmentation — segmentation often refers to dividing text into larger units like sentences.

- **Stemming vs Lemmatization**
  - **Stemming**: Reduces words to their base or root form, often by removing suffixes (e.g., "running" to "run").
    - **Mechanism**: Uses heuristic rules; can be aggressive and result in non-words.
    - **Importance**: Useful for reducing dimensionality but may lose meaning.
  - **Lemmatization**: Converts words to their base form using vocabulary and morphological analysis (e.g., "better" to "good").
    - **Mechanism**: Considers the context and part of speech.
    - **Importance**: Preserves meaning better than stemming.
  - **Common Confusion**: Many candidates think stemming and lemmatization are interchangeable; lemmatization is context-aware, stemming is not.

- **Stop Words**
  - **Definition**: Commonly used words (e.g., "and", "the") that are often removed from text data.
  - **Mechanism**: Identified and filtered out based on predefined lists.
  - **Importance**: Reduces noise and dimensionality but may remove context if not handled carefully.
  - **Red Flag**: Assuming stop words are universally applicable without considering context or domain.

- **Normalization**
  - **Definition**: The process of standardizing text data, including case normalization, accent removal, and expanding contractions.
  - **Mechanism**: Involves converting text to lowercase, removing diacritics, etc.
  - **Importance**: Ensures consistency in text data, crucial for accurate analysis.

## Bag of Words

- **Definition**: A representation of text data where each document is described by the frequency of words, disregarding grammar and order.
- **Mechanism**: Constructs a vocabulary from the corpus and represents each document as a vector of word counts.
- **Importance**: Simple and effective for text classification but ignores context and semantics.
- **Red Flag**: Misunderstanding that Bag of Words captures word order or semantics.

## TF-IDF Formula and Weighting

- **Definition**: Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency, a statistical measure used to evaluate the importance of a word in a document relative to a corpus.
- **Formula**: 
  - TF: \( \text{TF}(t, d) = \frac{\text{Number of times term } t \text{ appears in document } d}{\text{Total number of terms in document } d} \)
  - IDF: \( \text{IDF}(t, D) = \log\left(\frac{\text{Total number of documents } |D|}{\text{Number of documents containing } t}\right) \)
  - TF-IDF: \( \text{TF-IDF}(t, d, D) = \text{TF}(t, d) \times \text{IDF}(t, D) \)
- **Importance**: Highlights words that are important to a document but not common across the corpus.
- **Common Confusion**: Misinterpreting IDF as a measure of word frequency rather than rarity.

## Word Embeddings

- **Word2Vec (CBOW vs Skip-gram)**
  - **CBOW**: Predicts the target word from surrounding context words.
    - **Mechanism**: Uses context to predict a word, efficient for large datasets.
  - **Skip-gram**: Predicts surrounding context words from a target word.
    - **Mechanism**: Captures more semantic relationships, better for smaller datasets.
  - **Importance**: Captures semantic meanings and relationships between words.
  - **Red Flag**: Confusing CBOW and Skip-gram as identical in performance and application.

- **GloVe**
  - **Definition**: Global Vectors for Word Representation, an unsupervised learning algorithm for obtaining vector representations for words.
  - **Mechanism**: Constructs a co-occurrence matrix and factorizes it to obtain word vectors.
  - **Importance**: Captures global statistical information and semantic relationships.

- **FastText**
  - **Definition**: An extension of Word2Vec that considers subword information.
  - **Mechanism**: Represents words as n-grams of characters, allowing for better handling of rare words and misspellings.
  - **Importance**: Improves representation of morphologically rich languages and rare words.

## Transformer Architecture

- **Self-Attention**
  - **Definition**: Mechanism that relates different positions of a single sequence to compute a representation of the sequence.
  - **Mechanism**: Computes attention scores to weigh the importance of different words in a sequence.
  - **Importance**: Captures long-range dependencies and context effectively.

- **Multi-Head Attention**
  - **Definition**: Extends self-attention by using multiple attention mechanisms in parallel.
  - **Mechanism**: Allows the model to focus on different parts of the sequence simultaneously.
  - **Importance**: Enhances the model's ability to capture diverse patterns and relationships.

- **Positional Encoding**
  - **Definition**: Adds information about the position of words in a sequence to the input embeddings.
  - **Mechanism**: Uses sine and cosine functions to encode positions.
  - **Importance**: Provides the model with sequence order information, crucial for tasks like translation.

## BERT

- **Bidirectional**
  - **Definition**: Considers context from both directions (left and right) in a sequence.
  - **Mechanism**: Uses transformers to process text bidirectionally.
  - **Importance**: Captures more context than unidirectional models.

- **Masked Language Model**
  - **Definition**: Pre-training task where some words in a sentence are masked and the model learns to predict them.
  - **Mechanism**: Improves understanding of word context and relationships.
  - **Importance**: Enhances the model's ability to understand context and semantics.

- **Fine-Tuning**
  - **Definition**: Adapting a pre-trained BERT model to a specific task with additional training.
  - **Mechanism**: Involves training on task-specific data with task-specific layers.
  - **Importance**: Allows BERT to be applied to a wide range of NLP tasks with high performance.

## Text Classification

- **Definition**: The process of assigning categories or labels to text data based on its content.
- **Mechanism**: Uses algorithms like Naive Bayes, SVM, or neural networks to classify text.
- **Importance**: Essential for organizing and analyzing large volumes of text data.

## Named Entity Recognition (NER)

- **Definition**: Identifies and classifies named entities in text into predefined categories like names, dates, and locations.
- **Mechanism**: Uses rule-based, statistical, or neural network models to detect entities.
- **Importance**: Critical for information extraction and understanding text context.

## Evaluation

- **BLEU (Bilingual Evaluation Understudy)**
  - **Definition**: A metric for evaluating the quality of machine-translated text against reference translations.
  - **Mechanism**: Compares n-grams of the candidate translation to reference translations.
  - **Importance**: Widely used for evaluating translation quality.

- **ROUGE-N/L**
  - **Definition**: Recall-Oriented Understudy for Gisting Evaluation, measures overlap between candidate and reference summaries.
  - **Mechanism**: ROUGE-N measures n-gram overlap, ROUGE-L measures longest common subsequence.
  - **Importance**: Commonly used for evaluating summarization tasks.

- **Perplexity**
  - **Definition**: A measure of how well a probability model predicts a sample.
  - **Mechanism**: Lower perplexity indicates better predictive performance.
  - **Importance**: Used to evaluate language models, with lower values indicating better performance.

- **Red Flag**: Misinterpreting evaluation metrics as interchangeable without understanding their specific applications and limitations.
