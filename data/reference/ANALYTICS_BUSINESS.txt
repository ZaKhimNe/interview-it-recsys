## Cohort Analysis

- **Cohort Analysis**: A technique used to analyze the behavior of groups of users over time. Cohorts are typically defined by a shared characteristic or event, such as the month of user signup.
  - **Retention Cohort**: Focuses on tracking how many users continue to engage with a product over time after their initial interaction. This is crucial for understanding user engagement and product stickiness.
    - **Mechanism**: Users are grouped based on their start date, and their activity is tracked over subsequent periods. Retention rate is calculated as the percentage of users who return in each period.
    - **Importance**: Helps identify periods of high drop-off and assess the effectiveness of retention strategies.
    - **Common Confusion**: Confusing retention rate with churn rate. Retention measures continued engagement, while churn measures the rate of user loss.
  - **Behavior Cohort**: Groups users based on actions taken within the product, such as completing a tutorial or making a purchase.
    - **Mechanism**: Analyzes how specific behaviors impact long-term engagement and retention.
    - **Importance**: Identifies behaviors that correlate with higher retention or conversion rates.

## Funnel Analysis

- **Funnel Analysis**: A method to track the steps users take towards a conversion goal, identifying where drop-offs occur.
  - **Conversion Rates**: The percentage of users who complete a desired action out of the total who started the process.
    - **Formula**: Conversion Rate = (Number of Conversions / Total Visitors) * 100
    - **Importance**: High conversion rates indicate effective user journeys and product-market fit.
  - **Drop-off**: The point at which users abandon the process before completing the desired action.
    - **Mechanism**: Identifies stages in the funnel where users are most likely to leave, allowing for targeted improvements.
    - **Importance**: Reducing drop-offs can significantly improve overall conversion rates.
    - **Red Flag**: Misinterpreting drop-off as churn. Drop-off occurs within a funnel, while churn is a broader metric of user loss.

## RFM Analysis

- **RFM Analysis**: A marketing technique used to quantify customer value based on three dimensions: Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value.
  - **Recency**: How recently a customer made a purchase.
  - **Frequency**: How often a customer makes a purchase.
  - **Monetary**: How much money a customer spends.
  - **Mechanism**: Customers are scored on each dimension, and these scores are combined to segment customers into different value tiers.
  - **Importance**: Helps tailor marketing strategies to different customer segments, improving retention and sales.
  - **Common Confusion**: Confusing RFM with demographic segmentation. RFM is behavior-based, not demographic-based.

## Retention Metrics

- **Retention Metrics**: Metrics that measure how well a company retains its customers over time.
  - **Customer Retention Rate**: The percentage of customers who continue to use a product over a specific period.
    - **Formula**: Retention Rate = ((E-N)/S) * 100, where E = number of customers at end of period, N = number of new customers acquired during period, S = number of customers at start of period.
  - **Importance**: High retention rates are indicative of customer satisfaction and product value.
  - **Red Flag**: Using retention rate without context, such as industry benchmarks or historical data, can lead to misleading conclusions.

## Churn Prediction

- **Churn Prediction**: The process of identifying customers who are likely to stop using a product or service.
  - **Mechanism**: Utilizes historical data and machine learning models to predict churn likelihood.
  - **Importance**: Allows companies to proactively engage at-risk customers and reduce churn.
  - **Red Flag**: Overfitting churn prediction models to historical data without considering changing market conditions or user behavior.

## Business KPIs

- **DAU/MAU/WAU**: Metrics that measure user engagement over different time frames (Daily, Monthly, Weekly Active Users).
  - **Importance**: Provides insights into user engagement trends and product health.
  - **Common Confusion**: Misinterpreting these metrics as retention metrics. They measure activity, not retention.
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)**: The total revenue expected from a customer over their lifetime.
  - **Formula**: LTV = (Average Purchase Value) x (Average Purchase Frequency Rate) x (Customer Lifespan)
  - **Importance**: Helps determine how much can be spent on customer acquisition while remaining profitable.
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**: The cost associated with acquiring a new customer.
  - **Formula**: CAC = (Total Cost of Sales and Marketing) / (Number of New Customers Acquired)
  - **Importance**: Essential for assessing the efficiency of marketing strategies.
- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**: The average revenue generated per user.
  - **Formula**: ARPU = (Total Revenue) / (Number of Users)
  - **Importance**: Provides a quick snapshot of revenue performance per user.

## Attribution Modeling

- **Attribution Modeling**: The process of assigning credit to different touchpoints in the customer journey that lead to a conversion.
  - **First-Touch Attribution**: Assigns all credit to the first interaction.
    - **Importance**: Useful for understanding how initial interactions drive awareness.
  - **Last-Touch Attribution**: Assigns all credit to the last interaction before conversion.
    - **Importance**: Highlights the final steps that lead to conversion.
  - **Multi-Touch Attribution**: Distributes credit across multiple touchpoints.
    - **Importance**: Provides a more holistic view of the customer journey.
    - **Red Flag**: Ignoring the complexity of multi-touch models can lead to oversimplified conclusions.

## Customer Segmentation

- **Customer Segmentation**: The process of dividing a customer base into distinct groups based on shared characteristics.
  - **Mechanism**: Utilizes demographic, psychographic, and behavioral data to create segments.
  - **Importance**: Enables personalized marketing strategies and improves customer engagement.
  - **Common Confusion**: Confusing segmentation with personalization. Segmentation groups customers, while personalization tailors experiences to individuals within those groups.
